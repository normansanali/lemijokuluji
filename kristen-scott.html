Career

An English actress with dual French citizenship. She was a five-time BAFTA and Olivier Award nominee, winning the BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role for Four Weddings and a Funeral (1994) and the Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role for The Seagull in 2008. She was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Actress for her performance in The English Patient (1996).

Scott Thomas made her film debut in Under the Cherry Moon (1986), and for A Handful of Dust, she received the Evening Standard Film Award for Most Promising Newcomer (1988).

Bitter Moon (1992), Mission: Impossible (1996), The Horse Whisperer (1998), Gosford Park (2001), The Valet (2006), and Tell No One (2007) are among her films (2007). She received the European Film Award for Best Actress for her performance in Philippe Claudel’s I’ve Loved You So Long (2008). Leaving (2009), Love Crime (2010), Sarah’s Key (2010), Nowhere Boy (2010), The Woman in the Fifth (2011), Only God Forgives (2013), Darkest Hour (2017), and Tomb Raider (2017) are among her other films (2018).

For her contributions to drama, she was made an Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE) in the 2003 Birthday Honours and Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire (DBE) in the 2015 New Year Honours.
